John's Meatballs

from chef John Stilley

This meatball recipe, from chef John Stilley, makes some of the best meatballs I have ever had. We made them once together when I was about 12 years old in the kitchen of my old apartment. The smell of the meatballs pleasently filled my kitchen for days after we made them. Under this description, I have included a list of ingredients to make the meatballs, as well as step by step procudes. Underneath, the same for his brilliant sauce, which also makes some to freeze for future times. Enjoy!

Ingredients Meatballs

  • 2 pounds ground beef
  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 1 medium onion diced
  • 4 clover garlic minced
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 Tsb Morton Kosher Salt
  • 1 Tsb onion powder
  • 1 Tsp garlic powder
  • 1 Tbl spoon crushed Fennel seeds
  • 2 Tsp dry Italian seasoning
  • 1 Tsp dry Basil
  • Pinch Red Pepper flakes or to taste
  • 1 Tbls fresh chopped parsley
  • 1 cup Panko bread crumbs
  • ½ cup Parmigiano Reggiano freshly grated (more for topping finished dish)

Method

  1. Lightly Sautee the onion and garlic in olive oil. Don’t let it brown, just until softened. Let it cool to room temperature.
  2. In a large mixing bowl mix all ingredients including onion/garlic mixture. Be sure to mix eggs completely first.
  3. At this point I cook in a Sautee pan a small amount to check for salt/seasoning
  4. Roll away into golf ball sized meat balls. Roast on a sheet pan at 425 degrees or pan Sautee until slightly browned all over. This should give you plenty for a dinner with extra for the freezer. Store in a zip lock type bag.

Ingredients Sauce

  • ½ Large onion
  • 4 cloves garlic – minced
  • 2 Tbls olive oil to sautee.
  • 1 - 28-ounce San Marzano whole tomatoes
  • 1 - 8-ounce quality tomato sauce
  • 1 Tbls Italian seasoning
  • 1 Tbls Basil
  • Pinch red pepper flakes.
  • 1 Tsp Morton Kosher Salt
  • 1 Tbls Fresh parsley
  • ¾ Cup Red Wine
  • Fresh Basil for a gourmet finish topping.
  • Freshly grated Parmigiano Reggiano as well.

Method

  1. Sautee the onions and garlic until softened
  2. Add next 8 ingredients
  3. Crush the tomatoes with a potato masher until broken up, not obliterated.
  4. Simmer on low for 20 minutes. If it gets to thick and a little water.
